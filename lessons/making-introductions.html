Making Introductions

Introduction

Welcome to this Conversation Starters lesson. In this lesson, you'll learn phrases for introducing people in different social contexts. The phrases you use vary depending on the level of formality and whether you're introducing one person, multiple people, or two people to each other. First study the introduction phrases below, then practice determining which introduction is appropriate for different social situations.

Instructions

Familiarize yourself with the introduction phrases above. Practice pronouncing them aloud or in your head. Notice how the level of formality changes based on your relationship with the people involved.

When you're ready, click "Next Scenario" to see a social situation. Determine which introduction phrase would be most appropriate, then click "Show Answer" to check your response.

Key considerations:

  • Te presento a... - Use when introducing someone to a friend, peer, or someone you use "tú" with (informal)
  • Le presento a... - Use when introducing someone to an authority figure, elder, or someone you use "usted" with (formal)
  • Les presento a... - Use when introducing someone to a group of people
  • Les presento (el uno al otro/la una a la otra/le une a le otre) - Use when introducing two people to each other simultaneously
