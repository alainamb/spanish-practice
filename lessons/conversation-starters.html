Conversation Starters

Introduction

Welcome to this Conversation Starters lesson. In this lesson, you’ll learn repetitive phrases that people use when meeting for the first time and greeting one another. First study the conversation starters that you’ll use frequently in conversation, which vary depending on the level of formality of the social situation. Then get some practice determining which response are appropriate for different formality levels.

Instructions

Familiarize yourself with the conversation starters. In this activity, we’re focusing specifically on exchanges of “How are you’s?” Practice pronouncing the phrases in the chart aloud or in your head. Notice how the phrases go together. Imagine situations in which you’d use each expression.

When you’re ready, generate a random “How are you” coversation. The conversation generated is between two people:

  • Person 1: Asks a variation of “How are you?”
  • Person 2: Responds and asks a variation of “How are you?” back
  • Person 1: Responds with either positive or negative agreement

Review the conversation that was generated. Given the levels of formality and the phrases used, imagine a possible context for the conversation. Be specific about the context.

For example:

  • Persona 1: Hola. ¿Cómo estás?
  • Persona 2: Todo bien. ¿Y tú qué tal?
  • Persona 1: Yo también.

Analysis: The two speakers know one another well. They are coworkers. This is how they greet each other one morning when they get to work.
