Present + Preterite Tense Verbs - Conjugation Drill

Introduction

In this present plus past tense verb conjugation drill, you'll get practice conjugating a verb for a subject in the present tense. Then you'll convert your present tense sentence into the preterite tense.

Instructions

Select a category of verbs to practice. A subject and a infinitive verb will be displayed in Spanish. Conjugate the verb in present tense for the subject indicated. Then convert your conjugation to the preterite tense.

Remember:

  • Spanish has two past tenses: the preterite and the imperfect. The imperfect is a past tense that is used to talk about activities and states that were habitual or repetitive with no definite end. Here, we're practicing the preterite, or the past tense for activities and states that happened once or that had a specific ending.
  • You're encouraged to invent a sentence around the subject and the infinitive verb.

For example:

  • Question: yo + bailar → Response: Yo bailo los sábados and then Yo bailé el sábado pasado
  • Question: tú + bailar → Response: Bailas bien and then Bailaste bien el sábado
