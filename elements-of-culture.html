Elements of Mexican Culture

Introduction

Welcome to Elements of Mexican Culture. The topics and activities presented here will help you to develop cultural understanding about Mexico and her people as you learn the Spanish language. These materials represent an imperfect starting point and will at least give you an idea of some cultural elements that you could explore futher.

Click on the topic to learn more about that element of culture.

WHY MEXICO?

The Spanish courses taught by Alaina Brandt focus in particular on Mexican Spanish, since Alaina is an American living and working in Mexico, and Mexico and the United States are such close neighbors. Many of the classes that Alaina teaches end with cool down in which we spend 5-10 minutes discussing some element of Mexican culture. Some of the topics we've covered are introduced on this page.

WE WELCOME YOUR FEEDBACK

We welcome your feedback. You can contact us with your comments on the content and to suggest specific elements of culture that we could cover.

Geography

From mountains to forests to Carribean playas with ocean views of pristine turquoises and blues, Mexico has a diverse landscape. Here are elements of society and culture related to Mexico's geography.

These are some key points to get you started learning about Mexico's geography:

  • Mexico is in North America and its northern border is with the United States.
  • Although it's proximity to South America and the equator may make you expect warm climates all around, more mountainous regions of the country are quite rainy and temperate. Then again, desert regions get very hot and dry!

Understanding Mexico's geographic diversity will help you better appreciate regional variations in Spanish vocabulary, customs, and cultural practices you'll encounter in your studies.
