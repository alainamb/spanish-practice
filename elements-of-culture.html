Elements of Mexican Culture

Introduction

The Spanish courses taught by Alaina Brandt focus in particular on Mexican Spanish, since Alaina lives and works in Mexico, and Mexico and the United States are close neighbors. Many of the classes that Alaina teaches end with a 5-10 presentation of some element of Mexican culture. These presentations are introduced and organized into categories on this page. Click on the topic to access the full narrative and research associated with any of the element of culture presented.

Feel free to explore these elements of culture on your own. If you're taking a class with Alaina, we'll decide together which element of culture to cover in class.

We welcome your feedback. You can contact us to share resources or to request that a specific element of culture be covered on this page.

Geography

From mountains to forests to Carribean playas with ocean views of pristine turquoises and blues, the landscape in Mexico has a great deal of variety. Here are elements of culture related to Mexico's geography.

These are some key points to get you started learning about Mexico's geography:

  • Mexico is in North America and its northern border is with the United States.
  • Although it's proximity to South America and the equator may make you expect warm climates all around, more mountainous regions of the country are quite rainy and temperate.

Understanding Mexico's geographic diversity will help you better appreciate regional variations in Spanish vocabulary, customs, and cultural practices you'll encounter in your studies.
